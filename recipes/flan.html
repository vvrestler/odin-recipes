Flan

Description

Flan is a caramel topped custard that originated from Southern Europe. It is now very popular in Latin America.

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup sugar (for caramelizing the molds)
  • 1/3 cup water
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 1 1/3 cup milk
  • 1 1/3 cup half-and-half
  • 3 eggs
  • 5 egg yolks
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons vanilla

Instructions

  1. Using six 6-ounce molds, place them in a baking pan at least 2 inches deep
  2. Place 3/4 cup sugar and water in a small saucepan, bring to a boil, continue cooking until an amber color
  3. Immediately and carefully pour the hot sugar into the molds
  4. Heat the oven to 325 degrees fahrenheit
  5. In a medium saucepan, combine remaining sugar, milk, and half-and-half. Over medium heat, stir until it comes to a simmer
  6. In a large bowl, whisk the eggs and egg yolks, slowly pour in warm milk
  7. Stir in vanilla
  8. Strain through a fine mesh strainer, as you pour or ladle the custard into the molds
  9. Add simmering water to the baking pan to create a hot water bath for the molds
  10. Bake 50 - 60 minutes or until barely set in the middle
  11. Let the custards cool in the hot water bath, which takes about an hour
  12. Refrigerate 2 hours before serving
  13. To serve, run a small knife around the top edge of each flan, place a serving plate on top of the mold, holding them together tightly, turn them over, and give a shake until you hear the flan plop onto the plate
